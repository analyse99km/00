ZENO ORGANISM: DEEP AUDIT EXTRACTION WORKFLOW

WHAT IT WILL DO (AND WHAT IT EXTRACTS):
Zeno operates as a high-volume, deep-audit intelligence gatherer based on your configuration (currently targeting "crypto startups"). 

1. Target Discovery & Quota Routing
It constantly searches for high-signal discussions regarding the target topic. It builds a queue of accounts/startups discussing the topic until it hits a mandatory minimum quota (e.g., 100 accounts). 

2. Deep Account Dossier Extraction (Example Breakdown)
Imagine Zeno discovers a new crypto startup account. Here is exactly what it extracts and saves for that single account:

A. The Profile Snapshot (`profile.json`)
- The startup's handle, display name, and bio description.
- Discovery metrics (where Zeno found them and why).
- Account source URL and collection timestamps.

B. The Posts (`posts.jsonl`)
Zeno extracts up to 100 of the startup's public posts. For every single post, it saves:
- The full raw text of the post.
- Explicit Engagement Metrics: Exact counts of likes, retweets, replies, quotes, views, and bookmarks.
- Parsed Entities: All hashtags, cashtags (e.g., $BTC), @mentions, and external URLs extracted into a clean list.
- Media Attachments: Details of any images or videos attached to the post.
- Timestamps and URLs for the exact post.

C. The Community Comments (`comments.jsonl`)
For every post Zeno extracts, it pulls up to 50 replies/comments left by the public. For each comment, it saves:
- The text of the comment left by the community.
- The handle of the person commenting.
- Engagement metrics on the comment itself (likes, retweets, views).
- The parent tweet ID and conversation thread.

D. Traction Snapshots (`metric_snapshots.jsonl`)
- A time-series log of engagement counts. If Zeno sees the same post again later, it records the new metrics so you can track how fast a post is growing over time.

3. Infinite Discovery (No Repeats)
Every single account it fully audits is permanently memorized in its database. When Zeno runs again, it will instantly skip any account it already built a dossier for. It strictly focuses on discovering NEW startups.

WHERE IT SAVES THE DATA:
1. The Local Dossier Vault (`data/deep_audit/`)
Instead of dumping data randomly, Zeno builds a highly organized intelligence folder on its local machine:
- It creates global databases (`accounts.jsonl`, `posts.jsonl`, `comments.jsonl`, and `metric_snapshots.jsonl`) so you can search across all startups at once.
- It creates a dedicated folder specifically for this startup: `data/deep_audit/accounts/<handle>/` containing isolated copies of all the posts, profiles, and comments listed above. 
- It generates a human-readable `summary.md` detailing the entire operation.

2. The Permanent Target Repository
After the deep audit is 100% complete and the dossiers are built, Zeno immediately takes the entire `data/deep_audit/` folder and pushes it to your permanent vault:
https://github.com/analyse99km/project1-crypto_startups

Once the data is safely secured in your target repository, Zeno goes to sleep until its next run.
